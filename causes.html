Causes of Cardiovascular Disease:

1. Atherosclerosis: Atherosclerosis is the buildup of plaque (a combination of cholesterol, fat, and other substances) on the inner walls of arteries, leading to narrowing and hardening of the arteries. This restricts blood flow and increases the risk of heart attack and stroke.

2. Hypertension (High Blood Pressure): High blood pressure puts extra strain on the heart and blood vessels, increasing the risk of heart disease, stroke, and other cardiovascular complications.

3. Dyslipidemia: Dyslipidemia refers to abnormal levels of lipids (such as cholesterol and triglycerides) in the blood. High levels of LDL cholesterol (“bad” cholesterol) and low levels of HDL cholesterol (“good” cholesterol) increase the risk of atherosclerosis and cardiovascular disease.

4. Smoking: Tobacco smoke contains harmful chemicals that damage blood vessels, increase blood pressure, and promote the formation of plaque in the arteries, leading to an increased risk of heart disease and stroke.

5. Diabetes: Diabetes is a metabolic disorder characterized by high blood sugar levels. Uncontrolled diabetes can damage blood vessels and nerves, increasing the risk of cardiovascular disease, particularly coronary artery disease.

6. Obesity and Sedentary Lifestyle: Obesity and lack of physical activity are major risk factors for cardiovascular disease. Excess body weight, particularly around the abdomen, increases the risk of high blood pressure, diabetes, and dyslipidemia.

7. Unhealthy Diet: Consuming a diet high in saturated fats, trans fats, sodium, and processed foods increases the risk of cardiovascular disease. A diet low in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins deprives the body of essential nutrients needed for heart health.
